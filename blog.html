Understanding the Black-Scholes Equation

The Black-Scholes model provides a theoretical estimate of the price of European-style options. The fundamental partial differential equation is given by:

$$ \frac{\partial V}{\partial t} + \frac{1}{2}\sigma^2 S^2 \frac{\partial^2 V}{\partial S^2} + r S \frac{\partial V}{\partial S} - rV = 0 $$

In this post, we derive this equation using Ito's Lemma and discuss the assumptions behind the model, specifically the log-normal distribution of stock prices.
